Working Process

The workflow is designed to turn field needs into controlled records, responsible decisions, and auditable delivery steps.

Define requirement Clarify functional purpose, technical standard, quantity, site condition, schedule, acceptance evidence, and restricted information.
Screen route Compare local purchase, China sourcing, reuse, repair, and phased procurement alternatives.
Control supplier risk Check manufacturer capability, quality documents, export limits, sanctions concerns, and delivery reliability.
Record delivery Build traceable records from quote request, sample review, inspection, shipping, arrival, and issue correction.
Handover evidence Use checklists, photos, certificates, test reports, and site confirmations instead of verbal promises.

Quality documentation Every material or equipment batch should have document packages aligned with project acceptance needs. Missing nameplate, unverified certificate, inconsistent model, or unclear origin. Public examples only; raw certificates and project files remain restricted.
Logistics control Packing, shipping, customs, site arrival, storage, and defect response are treated as part of project delivery. Damage, missing parts, customs delay, storage loss, or handover dispute. Shipping documents and internal tracking remain restricted.

Responsible supply chain is a project-control function

The public value of this service is not simply procurement. It is the disciplined conversion of client needs into technical requirements, supplier screening, quality records, logistics controls, and delivery evidence.

This public page is not a supplier directory, price list, engineering drawing set, employment promise, or contract offer. Project execution requires lawful contracts, local licensing, safety conditions, insurance, tax and labor compliance, and appropriate professional review.
