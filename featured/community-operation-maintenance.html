Working Process

The workflow is designed to turn field needs into controlled records, responsible decisions, and auditable delivery steps.

Build asset list Identify facilities, equipment, spare parts, critical interfaces, warranties, and maintenance responsibilities.
Create routines Set daily, weekly, monthly, and seasonal inspection items with simple evidence records.
Train users Prepare practical instructions for local operators, volunteers, facility managers, and community stewards.
Track issues Use logs, photos, dates, responsible persons, corrective actions, and closure confirmation.
Review and improve Turn repeated defects into design, procurement, training, or operation improvements.

Record Area Public Decision Logic Main Risk Controlled Evidence Boundary
Facility handover Handover should include usable O&M records, not only completion photos or verbal instructions. Hidden defect, unclear responsibility, unsafe operation, or repeated repair cost. Public page shows method; actual handover packages remain restricted.
Preventive inspection Routine inspection is prioritized over emergency-only repair for critical community facilities. Small issue becoming shutdown, safety incident, or service interruption. Inspection templates may be shared after review.
Repair response Fault reporting, triage, temporary protection, repair action, and final confirmation should be documented. Uncontrolled repair, repeated failure, unsafe bypass, or dispute over responsibility. Issue logs and client details remain restricted.
Operator training Simple, local-language operation instructions reduce misuse and dependence on unavailable experts. Wrong operation, avoidable damage, or unsafe community use. Training materials can be provided to authorized partners.

Reconstruction does not end at completion

Communities need safe, usable, and maintainable facilities. This service focuses on the record-based transition from construction to daily operation, repair response, and long-term stewardship.
