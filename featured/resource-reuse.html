Working Process

The workflow is designed to turn field needs into controlled records, responsible decisions, and auditable delivery steps.

Inspect condition Review visible damage, age, service history, safety risk, contamination, missing documents, and remaining life.
Classify asset Mark as reuse, repair, test-before-use, restricted reuse, recycle, or disposal.
Verify safety Check load, pressure, electrical, fire, hygiene, code, and user-safety requirements before reuse.
Record decision Keep photos, notes, test results, responsible reviewer, reuse limits, and final disposition.
Control handover Confirm what was reused, what was replaced, what remains under observation, and what cannot be reused.

Material recovery Recovery is based on condition and safety, not simply on saving money. Unsafe reused material, hidden damage, contamination, or future failure. Public page shows method; project-specific inventory remains restricted.
Equipment reuse Electrical, mechanical, and control equipment require inspection and testing before reuse. Fire, leakage, electrical failure, warranty loss, or code non-compliance. Testing records and detailed asset lists are restricted.
Repair-or-replace Decisions compare immediate cost, life-cycle cost, availability, safety, and maintenance burden. False economy, repeated failure, or unsafe operation. Decision templates may be shared after review.
Environmental stewardship Reuse and recycling are documented as part of responsible reconstruction and resource stewardship. Waste, unmanaged disposal, or unclear accountability. Disposal and recovery records remain project-specific.

Reuse must be safe, documented, and responsible

Resource reuse is not a shortcut. It is a controlled engineering and stewardship process that protects safety, cost, environment, and accountability.
